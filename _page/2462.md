Are you new to Paleography? On this page you can familiarize yourself with basic characteristics of the main scripts of early Italian vernacular texts (most common abbreviations and letter forms) and make your own first transcriptions.

### Gothic

The Gothic script originated in university environments and mirrored a new way of studying: it favored speed in reading by combining clear subdivision of words and intense use of abbreviations. For more information about the characteristics and the history of the Gothic script see our [Handbook](handbook) page.

### Cancelleresca

The *cancelleresca* was a script typical of public (papal, imperial, regal or noble chancellery) and private (notarial) documents and was characterized by a cursive line. For more information about the characteristics and the history of the cancelleresca script see our <a href="https://centerfordigitalhumanities.github.io/Newberry-Italian-paleography/handbook">Handbook</a> page.

### Mercantesca

The mercantesca script developed in Tuscan mercantile environment and was the usual script of merchants, bankers, artists and working men. For more information about characteristics and history of the mercantesca script see our <a href="https://italian-paleography-dev.library.utoronto.ca/handbook">Handbook</a> page.

### Humanistic

The humanistic script first developed by a restricted Florentine cultural elite and later became the common script of scholars and nobleman. For more information about the characteristics and the history of the humanistic script see our <a href="https://centerfordigitalhumanities.github.io/Newberry-Italian-paleography/handbook">Handbook</a> page.

### Italic

The Italic script was a humanistic minuscule, semi-cursive hand. It would later be further develop with the invention of print and movable types. For more information about the characteristics and the history of the Italic script see our <a href="https://centerfordigitalhumanities.github.io/Newberry-Italian-paleography/handbook">Handbook</a> page.
