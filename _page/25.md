### Paleographical terms</h3>

**<a href="#A">A</a>**  ** <a href="#B">B</a>**   C   **<a href="#D">D</a> **  E   F   G   **<a href="#H">H</a>**   I   J   K   L   **<a href="#M">M</a>**   N   O   P   Q   R   **<a href="#S">S</a>**   T   U   V   W   X   Y   Z</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="A" name="A"></a>Abbreviations</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Used by scribes to save time and space while writing, abbreviations often take the form of contractions, with missing letters indicated by a mark or symbol; sometimes symbols replace entire words.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Autograph</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A text handwritten by its author.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="B" name="B"></a>Book hands</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Clear and regular scripts used in book production.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="D" name="D"></a>Documentary hands</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Scripts used for business and legal documents, letters, and other more ephemeral works; generally written rapidly and with less concern for clarity and regularity.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="H" name="H"></a>Hand</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

The writing of an individual scribe, with individual characteristics.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="M" name="M"></a>Majuscule</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Upper case or capital letters, all the same height.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Minuscule</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Lower case letters; they vary in their use of ascenders or descenders.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="P" name="P"></a>Paleography</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

The study of historical forms of writing.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="S" name="S"></a>Script</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

The type of writing a scribe is trying to emulate.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Strokes</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Single strokes of the pen; including the following:</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Arm</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A horizontal stroke that extends but does not cross the stem of letters such as E, F, and L.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Ascender</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A stroke that ascends above the body of a minuscule letter.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Bar</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A horizontal stroke that attaches two other strokes, in letters such as A and H.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Biting</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A stroke that is shared by two adjoining contrary curves, such as a b followed by an o; common in Gothic and Humanistic scripts.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Body</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

The part of a minuscule letter form that does not include an ascender or descender.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Bow (or lobe)</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A circle or partial circle attached to a stem, in letters such as p, b, q, and d.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Descender</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A stroke that descends below the body of a minuscule letter.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Ligature</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Two or more letters joined into a single glyph.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Lobe</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

See Bow</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Minim</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A simple vertical stroke with no ascender or descender.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Otiose stroke</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A random stroke on the page, not intended as punctuation or an abbreviation indicator.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Serif</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A small decorative line attached to the end of a stroke in some scripts.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Shading</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Variation in the thickness of strokes of a script.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Slant</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Variation of a stroke from complete vertical.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

     ⇒ Stem</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A minim with any ascender or descender that supports the rest of the letter.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="width:638px">

### Manuscript terms</h3>

A   B   **<a href="#c1">C</a>   <a href="#d1">D</a> **  E   **<a href="#f1">F</a>   <a href="#g1">G</a> **  H   **<a href="#i1">I</a> **  J   K   **<a href="#l1">L</a>   <a href="#m1">M</a> **  N   O   **<a href="#p1">P</a>   <a href="#q1">Q</a>  ** R  ** <a href="#s1">S</a>**   T   U   V   W   X   Y   Z</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="c1" name="c1"></a>Catchword</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A word (or phrase) written on the last page of a quire that matches the first word of the next quire, to assist the binder in assembling the quires in the correct order.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Codex</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A book made of folded sheets of paper or parchment stacked together, usually bound on one edge and enclosed within covers.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Colophon</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

An inscription made by the scribe, usually at the end of a book, recording information about the scribe and/or about how the book was produced.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="d1" name="d1"></a>Diplomatics</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Critical analysis of the conventions, protocols, and formulaic wording of types of historical documents.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="f1" name="f1"></a>Foliation</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Numbering of folios within a manuscript.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Folio</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A leaf of a codex.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="g1" name="g1"></a>Gathering</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

See Quire</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Gloss</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Commentary on or explanation of the main text, often written between the lines or in the margins.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="i1" name="i1"></a>Illumination</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Color illustrations or decorations in a manuscript, sometimes including gold or silver.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="l1" name="l1"></a>Line fillers</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Lines, symbols, or decoration used to complete a line of text not filled by script, essentially creating fully justified margins.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="m1" name="m1"></a>Manuscript</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Literally “written by hand.” Abbreviated as MS (singular) or MSS (plural).</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Marginalia</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Decoration or writing in the margins of a manuscript, either part of the original program or added by a later reader.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Miniature</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A stand-alone illustration in a manuscript.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Mise-en-page</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

The layout of text, columns, rubrication, decoration, images, and so forth on a page.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="p1" name="p1"></a>Page</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

One side of a folio within a codex.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Pagination</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Numbering of pages within a manuscript.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Paper</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A writing support usually made during the early modern period from chopped and soaked cotton or linen rags pressed in a frame.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Parchment</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A writing support made from processed, stretched, and dried animal skins, usually sheep, calf, or goat; originally “vellum” was reserved for calfskin, but the word is now used interchangeably with “parchment.”</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Pen flourishes</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Decorative fine lines made with a pen, emanating from initial or other letters, often in colored ink.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="q1" name="q1"></a>Quire</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

The sets of folded paper or parchment of which a codex is formed; also called a “gathering.”</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Quire signature</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

Identifying letters or numbers written within a quire, to assist the binder in assembling the folios in the correct order.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="r1" name="r1"></a>Recto</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

The front side of a folio within a codex.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Roll</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A book made of sheets of paper pasted or sewn together to form one long sheet, then rolled up for storage.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Rubric</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A heading or initial capitals written in a different color from the main text, in order to help distinguish the various textual components.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Running title</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

A line of text written at the top edge of a folio to identify a work’s title or a subsection of it.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="s1" name="s1"></a>Scribe</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

The individual who physically wrote a given document or book.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

<a id="v1" name="v1"></a>Vellum</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

See Parchment</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td style="width:157px">

Verso</p>
</td>
<td style="width:481px">

The back side of a folio within a codex.</p>
</td>
</tr></tbody></table>
